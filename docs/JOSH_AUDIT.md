## 1. Headline verdict

The collection is broad, genuinely no-fail, nicely personalized, and **strongest
exactly where Josh is strongest** ‚Äî the Find category (games that scale and theme
to his loves) and an unusually deep co-op bench (his #1 engagement lever). Nearly
every *headline* math working-edge has a dedicated, well-built game. **But it runs
easier than the 70/30 challenge/confidence target ‚Äî realistically ~55‚Äì60% true
challenge ‚Äî for two structural reasons, not because any single game is broken.**

1. **Almost nothing scales within a session.** Only the Find games and
   `number-muncher` get harder each round; the other ~50 multi-round games play
   round 5 at exactly round 1's difficulty. A curious 4.5-year-old who "gets it"
   in ten seconds still grinds four identical rounds.
2. **The confidence side is inflated by near-identical low-demand clones** ‚Äî 7
   science sorters that are the same tap-a-bin, 5 co-ops that are the same
   take-turns-tap-to-fill-a-meter, and ~5 count-to-10 games ‚Äî rather than a small,
   deliberate confidence slice.

The highest-leverage fix is **not** "add more games"; it's **make the challenge
games ramp, and consolidate/deepen the easy clones.**

## 3. Difficulty balance vs the 70/30 target

Of the ~72 teaching/challenge games (excluding ~5 pure delight/calm toys):
- **~55%** land at a true `[W]`/`[P]` challenge
- **~25%** are challenge-*intent* games that under-shoot to `[M]` (the 7 sorters, `order-num`/`order-size`/`shadow-match`, `which-more`, the count-to-10 cluster, weak `sight-word`)
- **~7%** cognitively-empty co-ops (5 tap-to-fill)
- **~13%** legitimate confidence/calm slice

**Verdict: ~55‚Äì60% challenge / 40‚Äì45% confidence ‚Äî under the 70% target, skewed easy.**
The miss is *concentrated*: removing the drag from the 7 identical sorters + 5
identical co-ops + `order-num` jumps the ratio most of the way to target. It's a
**distribution problem (too many clones of a few easy mechanics) plus a scaling
problem (challenge games don't ramp)** ‚Äî not a shortage of good hard games.

## 4. Coverage gaps ‚Äî thin `[W]` working edges
- **Sight words** ‚Äî one game, and it shape-matches instead of reading. *Weakest-covered edge.*
- **Writing his name** ‚Äî `letter-maker` traces L/V/O/C/U; **no game traces J-O-S-H.** *Gap.*
- **Count by 2s/5s** ‚Äî `skip-hop` only, flat, no 3s. *Thin.*
- **2-digit addition** ‚Äî `big-add` only, never regroups. *Thin at the top.*
- **The six individual continent maps** ‚Äî `continent-match` mixes all 7 with a self-check giveaway. *Gap.*
- **Geometry cabinets / plane shapes** ‚Äî only 3D solids covered. *Gap.*
- **Sharing / conflict-resolution SEL** ‚Äî implied by turn-taking only. *Thin.*
- **Over-served:** 7 `[M]` sorters, 5 tap-to-fill co-ops, ~5 count-to-10 games.

## 5. Prioritized recommendations (most impactful first)

1. **Make challenge games *scale*, using the Find category (`size = 9 + round*2`)
   as the template.** Add per-round ramps: `big-add` introduces a carry by round 4;
   `place-value` climbs past 40‚Üí99; `skip-hop` lengthens the run / hides two terms;
   the sorters move to trickier examples; `make-ten` graduates to 20. *One change
   pattern upgrades many existing `[W]` games at once ‚Äî biggest bang for the buck.*
2. **Rebalance the two over-weighted easy clusters instead of adding games.**
   (a) Let the 7 sorters scale to trickier/edge items; give one or two a 3-bin
   variant. (b) Layer a real skill onto 3 of the 5 tap-to-fill co-ops (as
   `team-sound-hunt`/`team-treasure` already do). *Fastest route from ~57% to ~70%
   challenge, and it turns his #1 engagement lever into a learning lever.*
3. **Fix `order-num` (and deepen `order-size`).** Randomize the run (4-7, or 12-15,
   or a missing-number-in-sequence); bump `order-size` to 4-5 items. *Near-zero
   effort, removes the worst offender, stretches number-sequence past 10.*
4. **Add a "Write My Name" tracer (J-O-S-H)** + friends' names. *Hits his literal
   writing edge AND the cheapest high-affect personalization lever.*
5. **Make `sight-word` a real reading task** ‚Äî speak the word only (or a short
   audio-supported phrase with the word missing) so he recognizes it rather than
   shape-matching. *Converts an `[M]`-plays game into a genuine `[W]` one.*
6. **Raise the ceiling on 2-digit addition and place value** ‚Äî `big-add` reaches a
   regroup in later rounds; `place-value` builds toward 99. *His headline math edges,
   both currently stopping short of his own mastered range.*
7. **Cover thin geography/geometry edges** ‚Äî a `continent-match` mode that names one
   continent without the animal-on-the-blob giveaway; a plane-shape game alongside
   `solid-match`.
8. **Turn co-op into a learning multiplier** ‚Äî add co-op *layers* to existing
   challenge games (2-player `big-add` or `rhyme` race, take-turns `picture-squares`)
   rather than a 6th meter-fill.
9. **(Minor) Age-up the toddler-leaning delight** ‚Äî a light counting/naming beat on
   `peekaboo`/`balloon`/`animals`.

---

*What's genuinely working and should be protected:* the Find category (scales +
themed + his favorite), the depth of co-op, the `[W]` logic quartet (`who-is-it`,
`birds-eye`, `picture-squares`, `story-order`), the new money games closing the old
coin gap, and the strong transformation-over-decoration design in `number-builder`,
`grow`, `landform-maker`, `piggy-bank`, `web-reveal`, and `thwip-web`. The site's
problem isn't quality ‚Äî it's that its many good hard games stay at one difficulty,
and a handful of easy mechanics are cloned too many times.
